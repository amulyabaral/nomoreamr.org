# Recommendations

Key recommendations for advancing integrated [[Antimicrobial Resistance (AMR)|AMR]] and [[Antimicrobial Use (AMU)|AMU]] surveillance in the Nordic region, based on the [[Roadmap]].

## 1. Harmonise Data Collection

- Adopt standardised data formats (WHO GLASS-compatible frameworks)
- Ensure all countries follow [[EUCAST]] guidelines consistently
- Develop shared protocols for sampling and susceptibility testing across [[One Health]] sectors

## 2. Preserve Data Granularity

- Store raw MIC and disk zone diameter data (not just S/I/R classifications)
- Enable retrospective analysis when [[EUCAST]] breakpoints change
- See [[AST Reporting]]

## 3. Connect Laboratory and Clinical Data

- Build secure systems linking bacterial isolates to patient diagnoses
- Leverage electronic medical records and health data registries
- Use unique patient identifiers with privacy protections
- See [[Clinical Isolate-Diagnosis Link]]

## 4. Enable Real-Time Sharing

- Develop near real-time data sharing for high-priority pathogen/drug combinations
- Build on existing national notification systems
- See [[Real-Time Data Sharing]]

## 5. Expand Surveillance Coverage

- Address [[Sampling Bias]] by including community settings and healthy populations
- Integrate [[Environmental AMR Surveillance|environmental monitoring]] (wastewater, water quality)
- Design complementary rather than overlapping surveillance networks

## 6. Address Legal and Regulatory Barriers

- Develop cross-border data-sharing frameworks compliant with GDPR
- Establish joint secure computing platforms for sensitive data
- Explore federated machine learning for joint analyses
- See [[Legal and Ethical Considerations]]

## 7. Invest in Training and Awareness

- Develop training activities for surveillance coordinators and researchers
- Disseminate evidence-based best practices
- Raise broader public awareness of AMR
