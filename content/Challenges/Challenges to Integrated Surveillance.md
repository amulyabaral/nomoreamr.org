# Challenges to Integrated Surveillance

Developing integrated [[Antimicrobial Resistance (AMR)|AMR]] surveillance across the Nordic countries involves ethical, legal, financial, and logistical challenges. Five key obstacles have been identified, along with proposed solutions.

## The Five Key Challenges

1. **[[Reporting of Aggregated Data]]** - Many surveillance systems only provide summaries, preventing re-analysis
2. **[[AST Reporting]]** - Loss of quantitative data when only S/I/R classifications are stored
3. **[[Clinical Isolate-Diagnosis Link]]** - Laboratory results not connected to patient records
4. **[[Real-Time Data Sharing]]** - Delayed reporting hinders outbreak detection
5. **[[Sampling Bias]]** - Data mainly from hospitals, not representative of broader populations

## Cross-cutting Themes

These challenges share common underlying issues:

- **Data granularity**: The tension between sharing detailed data and protecting privacy ([[Legal and Ethical Considerations|GDPR and legal constraints]])
- **Standardisation**: Need for harmonised protocols across countries (see [[EUCAST]])
- **Infrastructure**: Technical gaps in connecting laboratory, clinical, and public health IT systems
- **[[Secondary Data Sources]]**: Regulatory barriers to reusing existing data for AMR research

## Proposed Approach

The [[Roadmap]] proposes a phased approach:
1. Start with nationally aggregated data (avoiding GDPR complications)
2. Develop regulatory frameworks for raw anonymised data access
3. Build toward real-time, integrated data sharing across the [[One Health]] spectrum
