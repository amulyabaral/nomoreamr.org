# Norway

Norway operates a dual surveillance system: **NORM** for human AMR and **NORM-Vet** for veterinary and food AMR. The system is coordinated by the [[UNN|University Hospital of North Norway]] (NORM) and the [[NVI|Norwegian Veterinary Institute]] (NORM-Vet), with data published as a joint annual report.

## Human AMR Surveillance

- NORM established in 1999 to coordinate data from clinical microbiology laboratories nationwide
- [[UNN]] coordinates NORM from the Department of Microbiology and Infection Control
- [[NIPH]] manages the notification system and provides AMR/AMU data
- Protocol-defined yearly surveys of systemic and non-systemic bacterial and fungal pathogens (25+ years)
- High-priority resistance phenotypes submitted to designated reference laboratories
- A new **national microbiology database** containing all routine microbiology results is being developed
- Regulated under the Norwegian Infectious Disease Control Act (Smittevernloven, 1994)

> [!info] Recent development
> Norway published the **National One-Health Strategy against Antimicrobial Resistance (2024–2033)**, further developing AMR surveillance.

## Animal and Food AMR Surveillance

- **NORM-Vet** coordinated by [[NFSA]] and implemented by [[NVI]]
- Covers food, feed, and wild animals (broader scope than many countries)
- Systematic **MRSA screening of pig populations** - Norway has successfully eliminated livestock-associated MRSA from this reservoir
- Sales data from wholesalers and prescription data from the Veterinary Prescription Register

## AMU Monitoring

- **Human**: Norwegian Prescription Database managed by [[NIPH]] (all antimicrobials dispensed to outpatients). Hospital data from the **Sykehusapotekenes Legemiddelstatistikk** (hospital pharmacy drug statistics). National drug wholesale database covering all sales
- **Animal**: **VetReg** (Veterinary Prescription Register) managed by [[NFSA]]. Mandatory reporting for all food-producing animals and horses; voluntary for companion animals
- Standardised using ATC classification and DDDs (WHO Collaborating Center at NIPH)
