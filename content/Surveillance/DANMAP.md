# DANMAP

**DANMAP** (Danish Integrated Antimicrobial Resistance Monitoring and Research Programme) is [[Denmark]]'s integrated surveillance programme for monitoring [[Antimicrobial Resistance (AMR)|antimicrobial resistance]] and [[Antimicrobial Use (AMU)|antimicrobial consumption]] in humans, animals, and food.

- **Established**: 1995 - one of the world's first integrated AMR surveillance programmes
- **Website**: [danmap.org](https://www.danmap.org/)
- **Funding**: Ministry of Health and Ministry of Food, Agriculture and Fisheries

## Structure

DANMAP is a collaboration between:

- **[[SSI|Statens Serum Institut (SSI)]]** - Human clinical microbiology data, AMR/AMU analysis
- **[[DTU Food|National Food Institute (DTU Food)]]** - Animal and food AMR analysis
- **[[DVFA|Danish Veterinary and Food Administration (DVFA)]]** - Animal AMR/AMU data via VetStat
- **[[DHDA|Danish Health Data Authority (DHDA)]]** - Register of Medicinal Product Statistics
- **[[DK-Vet|DK-Vet Consortium]]** - Clinical isolates from sick animals

## Human Surveillance

| Component | Data Source |
|---|---|
| Clinical labs | [[SSI]] |
| Antibiotic sales | [[DHDA]] + [[SSI]] |

- Broad surveillance of invasive and non-invasive pathogens
- Integration of primary care data with hospital-level granularity
- Routine [[Whole-Genome Sequencing (WGS)]] for many pathogens
- Legislative basis: Danish Health Act + EU regulations

## Animal & Food Surveillance

| Component | Data Source |
|---|---|
| Pathogenic/zoonotic/indicator bacteria | [[DVFA]], [[DK-Vet]], [[DTU Food]] |
| Antibiotic sales | [[DVFA]], [[DTU Food]] |

- VetStat database captures detailed veterinary drug use data
- Indicator bacteria monitored beyond EU mandatory requirements
- Denmark monitors E. coli and enterococci in food-producing animals for data continuity

## Environmental Surveillance

- Pilot and research-based wastewater monitoring
- Not yet routine, but under development
- See [[Environmental AMR Surveillance]]

## Historical Significance

DANMAP was launched in 1995, following growing recognition of AMR as a cross-sectoral threat. The 1998 WHO/FAO/OIE conference in Denmark resulted in the **Copenhagen Recommendations**, emphasising coordinated surveillance - a landmark moment for global AMR awareness.

## International Reporting

DANMAP data feeds into:
- **EARS-Net** (human AMR)
- **EFSA/ECDC** (animal and food AMR)
- **ESAC-Net** (human AMU)
- **ESVAC/ESUAvet** (veterinary AMU)
- **WHO GLASS**
